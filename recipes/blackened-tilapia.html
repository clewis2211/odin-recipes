Blackened Tilapia

This Blackened Tilapia is one of my favorite fish recipes, and it’s super quick and easy. Mild tilapia fillets are dusted with a bold Cajun spice rub then sautéed in olive oil until crisp and flavorful on the exterior and tender and flaky within.


Ingredients

      1-1/2 teaspoons paprika
      1-1/2 teaspoons light brown sugar
      1 teaspoon dried oregano
      3/4 teaspoon garlic powder
      1/2 teaspoon cumin
      1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
      1 teaspoon salt
      4 six-ounce tilapia fillets
      1 lime, cut into wedges (optional, for serving)
      2 tablespoons olive oil, for cooking

  • Step 1

    Combine the paprika, brown sugar, oregano, garlic powder, cumin, cayenne pepper and salt in a small bowl

  • Step 2

    Mix until well combined (use your fingers to break up the lumps of brown sugar).

  • Step 3

    Sprinkle the spice rub evenly over both sides of the tilapia fillets.

  • Step 4

    Heat the olive oil over medium-high heat in a large non-stick pan.

  • Step 5

    Cook the fillets for 2-3 minutes on each side until reddish-brown on the outside and flaky on the inside.

  • Step 6

    Serve immediately with lemon wedges.
